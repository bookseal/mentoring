    US networking culture

    창발 is one Korean group — but why do Americans network so much?

    창발 (Changbal) is a Seattle non-profit (since 2014), 700+ members from Amazon, Microsoft, Google, with engineering / startup / design / women-in-tech subgroups.

    The bigger truth: in the US, 70–80% of jobs come through referrals and networks, not job boards. And it’s the weak ties — people you barely know — that bring the best chances, not close friends (Granovetter).

    check changbal.org weak ties / alumni (BU)

    • Why do people meet like this — is it really about referral hiring, or something else?

    LiDAR vs camera — let’s go deep

    You say cameras won’t work. The data says: it depends on the job.

    For grasping, the 2026 top models — π0.5, GR00T, Figure Helix, Tesla Optimus — are RGB-camera only, zero LiDAR.

    LiDAR (5–45 m, no color) is for driving / legs. Grabbing happens at 0.2–1 m, where you need dense color — a $344 RealSense gives that; a $1,200 LiDAR physically can’t. Your LiDAR view is right for mobile / defense robots — just not for hands.

    check Orbbec: LiDAR vs RGB-D our own writeup (knowledge/04)
    Ask Jay
    • When you say cameras won’t work — is that from the self-driving / mobile side?
    • For a curriculum about hands, do you agree camera-first is right?
    • Where would LiDAR actually earn its place in what we teach?

    J-1 visa — how to use it

    I can get one. How do I make it work for me?

    J-1 (exchange / trainee) can carry the 2-year home-country rule (INA 212(e)). If it applies, no H-1B and no green card until you serve 2 years back home — or get a waiver (no-objection letter, US agency interest, hardship).

    People use J-1 as a bridge, but 212(e) is the catch to check first.

    check state.gov: J-1 waiver USCIS I-612
    Ask Jay
    • Given my plan (Korea first, then foreign firms), does J-1 help me or trap me?
    • Is the 2-year rule a real wall, or waivable in a case like mine?
    • Have you seen good J-1 → longer-term paths?
